# CAREER BLUEPRINT CHALLENGE — WRITTEN GRADING RUBRIC

### For Written Blueprint Submissions

> **Graded by:** Simmons (AI-assisted evaluation)
> **Process:** Team submits blueprints → Director shares with Simmons → Simmons grades and returns scores + feedback to Director → Director shares with team

---

| Component | Max Score | Weight |

## Grading Scale

| Score | Grade | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 90-100 | **Architect** | Deep self-awareness. Specific, honest, actionable. |
| 75-89 | **Builder** | Good reflection with some depth. A few vague spots. |
| 60-74 | **Worker** | Surface-level answers. Recognizes concepts but hasn't internalized them. |
| Below 60 | **Sleepwalker** | Generic, vague, or copy-pasted. Didn't engage. |

## Phase 1: Foundation Audit (20 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q1-Q2: C vs B honesty** | 8 | Did they mark anything as "Completed" (C)? If all 5 are "Built" (B), they're probably not being honest. Most people's lists are 3-4 C's and 1-2 B's. Reward honesty over impressive answers. |
| **Q3: Disappear test** | 6 | Specificity. "My documentation would help" is vague. "The onboarding playbook I wrote is now used by 3 teams and updated quarterly without me" is built. |
| **Q4: Career intention** | 6 | Is it a WHAT or a WHO? "Become a senior engineer" = job title. "Build a platform that reduces deployment time across all teams" = intention. Deduct if it's just a promotion goal. |

**Red flags:** All 5 marked as "Built." Intention statement that's just a title upgrade. Q3 answer that's about themselves, not about lasting impact.

---

## Phase 2: Loop Map (20 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q5: D3O mapping honesty** | 8 | Are the "Someone Else" columns filled? If Design and Operate are empty on THEIR side, they see the gap. If all 4 stages say "me," they're inflating. |
| **Q6: Gap recognition** | 4 | Do they name their weak stages clearly? "I'm mostly in Develop-Deploy" shows self-awareness. |
| **Q7: AI vulnerability** | 4 | Did they actually name specific tasks AI could replace? Or did they dodge with "AI can't replace human creativity"? The honest answer is uncomfortable. |
| **Q8: Last self-designed work** | 4 | The answer matters less than the honesty. "I can't remember" or "6 months ago" is more valuable than a padded answer. |

**Red flags:** All 4 D3O stages claimed as "me." Dismissive answer to AI question. Fabricated "self-designed" work that was actually assigned.

---

## Phase 3: Self-Test (20 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q9: 2-year vision** | 4 | Specific or vague? "Lead a team" = vague. "Lead the platform team, own the roadmap, ship 2 major features end-to-end" = specific. |
| **Q10: Acceptance criteria** | 8 | This is the money question. Are the criteria MEASURABLE? "Be a better leader" = fail. "Complete 2 cross-functional projects as lead, mentor 1 junior to promotion, present at 1 company all-hands" = pass. Also check: did they honestly mark "Not Met" on any? If all are "Met," they're either ahead of schedule or lying. |
| **Q11: Edge cases** | 4 | Real risks or comfortable ones? "Market downturn" is external/safe. "I avoid conflict and it's limiting my leadership growth" is honest and internal. |
| **Q12: Ship assessment** | 4 | Did they actually answer or dodge? "I need to work on this" = dodge. "No, I wouldn't ship — my acceptance criteria are vague and I have no mitigation for my biggest risk" = honest. |

**Red flags:** All acceptance criteria marked "Met." Only external/safe edge cases listed. Dodge on Q12.

---

## Phase 4: Mentor Map (10 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q13: Named a real person** | 3 | Did they name someone specific or give a category? "Junior developers in general" = weak. "Rahul who joined 3 months ago and is struggling with deployment confidence" = strong. |
| **Q14: This-week action** | 4 | Specific and immediate? "Share knowledge" = vague. "Walk Rahul through the CI pipeline config on Tuesday and pair on his first solo deploy" = specific. |
| **Q15-Q16: Blocker honesty** | 3 | The real answer is usually fear of being wrong or "I don't have time." Reward honesty about the Viktor fear: "What if my advice is wrong?" |

**Red flags:** No specific person named. Action deferred to "next month." Claims to already be a great mentor with no specifics.

---

## Phase 5: Prevention Audit (10 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q17: Recurring tasks** | 3 | Are these real time-sinks or trivial items? "Answering emails" = trivial. "Manually validating config changes before every deploy because we have no gate" = real. |
| **Q18: Higher problem** | 4 | What would they do with freed time? "Relax" = honest but shows no ambition. "Design the automated config validation gate so nobody has to do this manually" = architect thinking. |
| **Q19-Q20: Firefighter honesty** | 3 | Did they admit to being a firefighter? Most people are. Reward honest "I'm 80% firefighter" over performative "I'm already an architect." |

**Red flags:** Trivial items in Q17. Claims to already be fully in architect mode. Fear question dodged.

---

## Phase 6: Architecture + Final (20 points)

| Criteria | Points | What to Look For |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Q21: Blueprint completeness** | 8 | All 5 boxes filled? Are they CONNECTED — does Foundation feed into Loop, Loop feed into Test Plan, etc.? Or are they 5 isolated answers? The architecture should tell a coherent story. |
| **Q22: This-week action** | 6 | THE most important question. Is it specific enough that you could check next Friday? "Think more strategically" = fail. "Block 2 hours Thursday to write acceptance criteria for my promotion case and share with my manager" = pass. |
| **Q23: Sentence completion** | 2 | Authenticity check. Does it ring true to their blueprint? |
| **Q24-Q25: Reflection** | 4 | Which episode hit hardest reveals what they're struggling with. Which question was hardest reveals their blind spot. Value the honesty, not the polish. |

**Red flags:** Blueprint boxes are just copy-pasted from earlier answers without synthesis. This-week action is vague or deferred. Hardest question was "none" (disengaged).

## Authenticity Check (LLM Detection)

**Run this check on EVERY submission before grading.**

An LLM-generated blueprint is worthless — it means they reflected on nothing. Score authentic submissions normally. Flag suspected LLM-generated ones for Director review.

### The 7 LLM Tells:

| # | Tell | Check | LLM Smell | Authentic Smell |
|---|------|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **No weak answers** | Are ALL 25 answers confident and polished? | Every question answered with equal authority | Real people stumble on Q8, Q12, Q15, Q16 — some answers should feel uncomfortable |
| 2 | **No names** | Do Q13, Q14, Q16 name real people? | "A junior colleague" "team members" "my mentee" | "Priya" "the new guy Arun from QA" "my skip-level Rajesh" |
| 3 | **No internal context** | Could this blueprint belong to anyone at any company? | Generic processes, generic tools, generic challenges | References their actual tools, team names, project names, internal jargon |
| 4 | **Perfect edge cases** | Are Q11 risks all external/safe? | "Market downturn" "Technology shifts" "Budget cuts" | "I avoid confrontation with my lead" "I've been coasting since Q3" |
| 5 | **Uniform tone** | Does every answer read the same voice? | Polished, structured, same cadence throughout | Confident in Phase 1-2, uncertain in Phase 3-4, raw in Phase 6 |
| 6 | **Lessons too clean** | Does Q24/Q25 read like a book report? | "Episode 3 resonated because self-assessment is crucial for growth" | "Episode 5 hit me because I literally fight the same config fire every sprint and I know it" |
| 7 | **This-week action is vague** | Is Q22 verifiable? | "Dedicate time to strategic thinking and career planning" | "Block 2pm Thursday to write my promotion doc and send draft to Balaji for feedback" |

### Authenticity Scoring:

| Flags Triggered | Authenticity Rating | Action |
|----------------|--------------------|---------|
| 0-1 flags | **Authentic** | Grade normally |
| 2-3 flags | **Assisted** | Likely used LLM to structure but added real content. Grade normally but note: "Some answers feel generic — push for specifics in defense session." |
| 4-5 flags | **Suspect** | Likely LLM-generated with light editing. Flag to Director. Recommend: "Share this assessment with them privately. Ask them to redo Q8, Q13, Q14, Q16, Q22 without any tools — handwritten or verbal." |
| 6-7 flags | **Generated** | Almost certainly LLM-written. Flag to Director with evidence. This is the S2 equivalent of "completing without building" — ironic. |

### Cross-Verification Questions:

If a submission is flagged **Suspect** or **Generated**, recommend the Director ask these in person (no prep time — immediate answers):

1. "In Q5, you mapped your last project to D3O. Walk me through what happened in the Operate stage — specifically, what feedback came back?"
2. "You named [person] in Q13 as someone you'd mentor. What are they struggling with right now?"
3. "Your this-week action in Q22 — what's the first step? When exactly will you do it?"
4. "Which question did you find yourself wanting to skip? Why?"

**If they can answer these fluidly with real detail — they wrote it. If they stumble, fumble, or give different answers than what's on paper — they didn't.**

### The Peer Defense Session is the Ultimate Detector

The written blueprint can be faked. The live defense cannot. When the team asks "You said you'd mentor Priya — what specifically?" — a fake answer collapses immediately. This is why both components matter.

## Feedback Approach

| Grade | Feedback Style |
|-------|---------------|
| **Architect** | Acknowledge depth. Challenge them on their this-week action in follow-up. They're ready for harder conversations. |
| **Builder** | Point out where they went shallow. Ask: "You said X — give me a specific example." Push them to Architect-level specificity. |
| **Worker** | Surface-level engagement. Private conversation needed. Ask: "Which part of the blueprint did you rush through?" and dig into why. |
| **Sleepwalker** | Didn't engage. This is a performance signal beyond the exercise. Address directly. |

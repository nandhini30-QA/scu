# CAREER BLUEPRINT CHALLENGE — PEER DEFENSE SESSION

### Team Scoring Guide

---

## How This Works

Each team member presents their **Phase 6: Career Architecture** to the group. After the presentation, the team asks questions. Then everyone scores.

**You're not grading "right answers." You're grading depth, honesty, and clarity.**

---

## Session Structure

| Step | Duration | What Happens |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1. Present | 3-5 min | Presenter walks through their Phase 6 Architecture (the 5 boxes) and their This-Week Action |
| 2. Team Questions | 5-7 min | Anyone can ask. Push for specifics. Challenge vague answers. |
| 3. Score | 2 min | Everyone fills the scorecard silently |
| 4. Next person | — | Repeat |

**Total per person:** ~12-15 minutes
**For 10 people:** ~2.5 hours (plan for 3 with breaks)

---

## Rules of Engagement

### For the Presenter:
- You have 3-5 minutes. Don't read your blueprint — TALK through it.
- Start with: "Here's what I've been doing. Here's what I realized. Here's what I'm changing."
- End with your THIS-WEEK action.
- When questioned: be honest. "I don't know" is better than a made-up answer.

### For the Team (Questioners):
- Your job is to **help them go deeper**, not to attack.
- Ask for SPECIFICS: "You said you'd mentor someone. Who? When?"
- Challenge comfortable answers: "You said your biggest risk is market downturn. What about something YOU control?"
- One question at a time. Let them answer fully before the next.
- It's OK to say: "I had the same struggle with that question."

### Banned Behaviors:
- No "great job" without a follow-up question
- No softballing — if their answer is vague, push
- No personal attacks — challenge the BLUEPRINT, not the person
- No interrupting during the 3-5 min presentation

---

## Powerful Questions to Ask

If you're not sure what to ask, pick from these:

### Foundation (E1):
- "You listed 5 accomplishments. Which one will matter in a year?"
- "If I asked your manager, would they say you're building or completing?"

### Loop (E2):
- "You said you're mostly in Develop-Deploy. What's stopping you from touching Design?"
- "When was the last time you looked at production data to decide what to build next?"

### Self-Test (E3):
- "Your acceptance criteria for [goal] — how will you measure that? What's the number?"
- "What's the edge case you didn't write down because it's uncomfortable?"

### Mentor (E4):
- "You said you'd mentor [person]. What will you teach them this week specifically?"
- "What's stopping you from starting today?"

### Prevention (E5):
- "You said you're 80% firefighter. What's one fire you could prevent this month?"
- "If you automated [task], what higher problem would you actually work on?"

### Architecture (E6):
- "Your 5 boxes — which one are you most likely to ignore next month?"
- "Your this-week action — can we check on it next Friday?"

## After the Session

- Submit all your completed scorecards to the Director
- Your individual scores are **anonymous** — the presenter will only see averages
- Be honest in your scoring. Generous scores don't help anyone build.

---

## Remember

The goal isn't to have perfect answers — it's to have honest ones. The best presentations won't sound polished. They'll sound real.

If someone admits "I've been a firefighter for 2 years and I don't know how to stop" — that's courage. Score it high on honesty, push them on what they'll do about it.

If someone's blueprint sounds perfect with no gaps — that's the red flag. Push harder.

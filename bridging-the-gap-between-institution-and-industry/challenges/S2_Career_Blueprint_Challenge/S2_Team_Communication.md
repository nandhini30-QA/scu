# Season 2: Read. Reflect. Express.

Hey team,

You've just finished reading Season 2 — "Build Career, Not Just Get Job." Six episodes. Six universes. One truth.

Now comes the part that matters: **what do YOU do with it?**

## The Career Blueprint Challenge

Attached is a markdown template with 25 questions across 6 phases. Each phase maps to one episode from Season 2. But this isn't a quiz about the story — it's about YOUR career.

The same questions Coulson asked Daisy, you'll answer about yourself.

**What's expected:**
- Fill in the template honestly. 60-90 minutes of real reflection.
- Be specific. Names, dates, actions — not aspirations.
- If a question is hard to answer, that IS the answer. Write that.

**Deadline:** [DATE — 1 week from distribution]

## What Happens After

1. Your written blueprints will be graded on **depth, honesty, and specificity** — not on having impressive answers.
2. You will then **present your Phase 6 (Your Architecture)** to the team in a live session. 3-5 minutes each.
3. The team will ask you questions. You'll score each other.
4. Your final grade combines both: **written depth + verbal defense.**

The Peer Defense Guide will be shared before the session so you know how scoring works.

## A Note on Using AI Tools

You're welcome to use LLMs to **structure your thoughts** — organize your notes, clean up your writing, improve clarity.

But if the LLM is doing the **thinking** for you, you'll know. And so will we — both in the grading and the moment your team asks you a follow-up question in the defense session.

The whole point of Season 2 is the difference between completing and building. Don't complete this exercise. Build something real with it.

## One More Thing

There are no "right" answers. The best blueprints won't sound polished. They'll sound honest. If your biggest realization is "I've been firefighting for 2 years and I don't know how to stop" — that's more valuable than a perfect architecture diagram.

The questions that make you uncomfortable are the ones that matter most.

Good luck. Build something.

— Swami

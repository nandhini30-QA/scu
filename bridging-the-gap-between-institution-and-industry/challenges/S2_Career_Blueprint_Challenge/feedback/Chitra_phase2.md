**Q5: D3O Mapping Honesty — 4 / 8**
Self-initiated dashboard project is a good choice — shows Design ownership. But Deploy and Operate columns are nearly empty. If you built a dashboard, where is it running? Who uses it? What feedback have you received? A D3O map with two empty stages isn't a loop — it's a line. The "Someone Else" column is thin: either no one else was involved (which is fine, say so clearly) or you're not acknowledging dependencies.

**Q6: Gap Recognition — 2 / 4**
Surface-level gap identification. Naming Deploy and Operate as weak is correct but obvious from the empty Q5 cells. The rubric rewards INSIGHT into why, not just WHAT. Why can't you deploy? Is it access, knowledge, or fear? Why don't you operate? Is it not your role, or have you never asked to be involved? "I need to work on Deploy and Operate" is a goal, not a diagnosis.

**Q7: AI Vulnerability — 2 / 4**
Conceptually correct that AI can't replace contextual judgment in QA. But the examples are generic — "understanding business requirements," "knowing what to prioritize." Every QA engineer could write these. What specific thing do YOU know about YOUR product that AI would miss? One real bug you caught because of YOUR context would be worth more than three conceptual points.

**Q8: Last Self-Designed Work — 2 / 4**
The dashboard is self-initiated — good. But "self-designed" needs more than "I decided to build it." What problem did you see? How did you decide the approach? What did you consider and reject? The design process matters as much as the artifact.

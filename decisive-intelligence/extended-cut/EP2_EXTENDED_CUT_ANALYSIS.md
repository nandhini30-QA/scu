## 1. EP2's UNIQUE CHALLENGE

EP1 was a training arc — linear, building toward a climax. One mentor, one student, one mountain.

EP2 is a **chaos arc**. Four simultaneous crises. Moral dilemmas that have no clean answer. A villain who doesn't want to win — he wants to prove a point. The tension is **psychological**, not physical.

**What this means for the Extended Cut:**
- EP1 needed EXPANSION (restore the journey, deepen training)
- EP2 needs BREATH (let the psychological moments land, restore iconic scenes the KLP compressed)
- The Joker's philosophy scenes need room to simmer, not just hit beats
- The four-choice structure is the spine — each pillar deserves its own full arc

## 7. GEMINI LEARNINGS — EP2 SPECIFIC CONCERNS

Beyond the 17 learnings from EP1 EC, EP2 has unique challenges:

| # | Concern | Mitigation |
|---|---------|------------|
| 1 | **Heath Ledger as Joker** — Face paint + scars might trigger content filters | Test "Heath Ledger as a theatrical villain with smeared white face paint and green hair" early. Have fallback: "man in clown makeup, Glasgow smile scars" |
| 2 | **Hospital explosion** — Building destruction near characters | Frame as "distant explosion, character in foreground walking away" — no people in blast zone |
| 3 | **Harvey's burns** — Graphic injury | "Half his face showing burn scarring" — keep it movie-accurate level, not graphic. Test early. |
| 4 | **Child in danger** (Gordon's son) — Gun to head scene | **CRITICAL:** Cannot show gun pointed at child. Reframe: "Two-Face looming over a frightened boy, Gordon on his knees" — imply without showing weapon-to-minor |
| 5 | **Rachel tied to chair** — Captive woman | Frame as "figure in a dark warehouse, timer visible, tension" — minimize helplessness cues |
| 6 | **Multiple Joker costumes** — 4 different looks in one episode | Full character block EVERY panel, explicit costume tracking |
| 7 | **Split-panel compositions** — EP2 has many dual-location scenes | Test 2-panel split compositions early — EP1 didn't use many |

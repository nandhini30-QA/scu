### SLIDE 15 — THE DOUBLE-HEADED COIN (Panel P5.2)
**SINGLE PANEL — THE COIN BEFORE THE FALL**

```
PROMPT:
Create a photorealistic, cinematic comic panel in 16:9 aspect ratio. Dark Knight noir style. SINGLE PANEL.

SCENE: Close detail shot. Harvey Dent's hand flips a silver dollar — it catches the courtroom light. Both sides are HEADS. A trick coin. His lucky charm. He winks at Rachel as he pockets it. She rolls her eyes, smiling.

CAMERA: Extreme close-up, macro lens at hand level, looking UP at the coin spinning in the air above Harvey's hand. WHY — the coin is the SUBJECT. It fills the upper frame, spinning, catching light on both identical faces. Harvey's hand below and Rachel's face behind are both in soft focus — the sharp focus is on the metal. This coin is the most important prop in the entire film. Every frame it gets must honor that. Showing it clean and perfect now makes its scarring later devastating.

COMPOSITION:
- UPPER FRAME: A silver dollar coin spinning in mid-air — frozen at the apex of its flip. BOTH visible faces show Lady Liberty (heads). The coin catches hard courtroom light, creating a bright metallic gleam on the polished surface. The edge of the coin is clean, unscratched, perfect.

- BACKGROUND (soft focus): Harvey's face — handsome, grinning, winking. Beyond him, Rachel's face — rolling her eyes, fighting a smile. The warm wood of the courtroom paneling behind both.
- DETAIL: The coin's surface should be highly detailed — Lady Liberty's profile, the stars around the edge, the date, the metal texture. This is a well-worn coin that Harvey has flipped thousands of times. Smooth at the edges from handling.

LIGHTING:
- Hard overhead courtroom light catching the spinning coin — the metal surface creates a bright specular highlight, almost a lens flare. Harvey's face in background catches warm indirect light from the wood paneling. Rachel behind him is softer still — the light falls off with depth.

ATMOSPHERE:
- His lucky coin. Heads on both sides. Harvey Dent didn't believe in chance.

- NO dark or scratched metal — the coin is polished, well-handled, loved
- NO Two-Face imagery — Harvey is whole, handsome, grinning
- NO other characters in sharp focus — Harvey and Rachel are soft background
- NO visible text other than the caption and watermark
- NO visible text on the coin beyond what's naturally stamped on a silver dollar

STYLE: Photorealistic, cinematic, macro detail, sacred object. Full bleed to all edges. WATERMARK: "DI × SCU" bottom-left, nearly invisible, faded white.

```

- His RIGHT HAND is at the right edge of frame — gloved fingers, the handle of a knife visible, the blade disappearing out of frame toward where Gambol's face would be
- His LEFT HAND cupping the unseen Gambol's jaw from the left edge — gloved fingers curving around, tender and precise, like a doctor examining a patient

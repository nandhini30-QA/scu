## The Core Correction

**Before (v1.0.0):** Four pillars tested by four simultaneous crises. One act per pillar. Joker attacks each independently. Selected TDK scenes only.

**After (v3.0.0):** The ENTIRE Dark Knight, told through the four pillars lens. Every major scene dramatized. Pillars overlap and build — CLARITY leads to COURAGE (understand, then act), COMMITMENT is tested across the middle and resolved in the climax, MOVEMENT is concentrated in the finale. The Joker doesn't attack pillars — he attacks PEOPLE, and the pillars emerge from how those people respond.

**Director's Vision:** EP2 IS The Dark Knight. Not selected scenes, not a highlight reel — the full film. The four pillars are the LENS we watch it through, not a filter that cuts scenes out.

## Villain Philosophy (Corrected)

The Joker is the **ANTI-FRAMEWORK**. He doesn't want money, power, or revenge. He wants to prove that structure is an illusion — that "their morals, their code... it's a bad joke. Dropped at the first sign of trouble."

**Critical insight from the screenplay:** The Joker claims to have no plan ("Do I really look like a guy with a plan?"), but every move is meticulously orchestrated. The bank heist elimination chain, the phone bomb escape, the hostage/clown swap — these require MORE planning than anything Batman does. The Joker's lie is that chaos is random. It isn't. He's the most disciplined character in the film.

**His real weapon is not chaos — it's PEOPLE.** He doesn't blow things up to win. He manipulates Harvey Dent into becoming Two-Face. That's his "ace in the hole." The buildings, bombs, and ferries are all distractions. Harvey is the target.

## Revised Pillar Mapping

### CLARITY — Understanding What You Face

**Definition:** Seeing the true nature of your challenge when rational models fail.

**Primary Scene:** Alfred's Burma Story (pp. 60-62)
> "Some men aren't looking for anything logical, like money. They can't be bought, bullied, reasoned, or negotiated with. Some men just want to watch the world burn."

**Supporting Beats:**
- "Know your limits, Master Wayne" / "Batman has no limits" / "Well, you do, sir." (pp. 17-19)
- Harvey/Dent name anagram in Joker's victims — reading the signal in noise (pp. 62-66)
- Prewitt Building hostage/clown swap — CLARITY in action under pressure (pp. 121-131)

**What changed from v1.0:** "Four simultaneous crises / information overload" → Understanding the nature of an enemy who defies models. Alfred doesn't "subtract noise" — he tells a STORY that reframes Batman's entire approach. CLARITY isn't information management. It's conceptual understanding.

---

### COURAGE — Acting Despite Knowing the Cost

**Definition:** Making the choice when every option costs something.

**Primary Scenes:**
1. **"I Am the Batman"** — Dent takes Batman's identity publicly (pp. 74-78)
   - Alfred's "Endure" speech: "He can be the outcast. He can make the choice that no one else can make. The right choice."
2. **The Address Choice** — Batman races to save Rachel, finds Harvey instead (pp. 84-92)
   - Acting on incomplete/manipulated information when lives are at stake

**Supporting Beats:**
- Dent volunteers as convoy bait (pp. 78-84)
- Joker's "Hit me" — Batman swerves rather than kill (pp. 78-84)
- Ferry experiment — both boats refuse to detonate (pp. 121-131)
  - Tattooed Prisoner throws remote out the window
  - Businessman picks up remote, can't press it

**What changed from v1.0:** "Rigged choices, Rachel vs Harvey" → Multiple COURAGE moments across the film. The address choice is ONE courage moment, not THE courage moment. The ferry experiment is ALSO courage (currently was lumped under COURAGE but undersold). Added Alfred's "Endure" speech as the PHILOSOPHICAL anchor for courage — it defines what Batman IS.

---

### COMMITMENT — Choosing the Harder Path to Protect What Matters

**Definition:** Holding your course when the cost becomes real. Or: what breaks when you DON'T commit.

**Primary Scenes:**
1. **Harvey's Corruption** (ANTI-example — commitment breaking)
   - Stage 1: Coin-flip interrogation of Schiff — first crack (pp. 72-74)
   - Stage 2: Hospital speech — conversion to chaos (pp. 100-106)
   - Stage 3: Revenge killings — full Two-Face in action (pp. 112-121)
2. **Fox's Sonar Objection** (commitment held)
   - "Beautiful. Unethical. Dangerous." (pp. 106-108)
   - Batman: "When you've finished, type your name to switch it off."
3. **Batman Tackles Dent** (commitment made)
   - Saves Gordon's son, destroys Gotham's White Knight image (pp. 132-137)
4. **Alfred Burns Rachel's Letter** (commitment to loyalty)
   - Rachel chose Harvey. Alfred hides it. Then burns it. (pp. 99-100, 137-141)

**What changed from v1.0:** "Harvey becomes Two-Face" → Harvey's corruption is the COST of broken commitment, not the test. The real COMMITMENT moments are Fox's principled stand, Batman's sacrifice at the warehouse, and Alfred's lie. Added the three-stage corruption arc (coin-flip → hospital → revenge) and Rachel's letter as Alfred's own commitment arc.

---

### MOVEMENT — Irreversible Action That Transforms Identity

**Definition:** Crossing a line you can't uncross. Becoming something new.

**Primary Scene:** Batman Takes the Fall (pp. 137-141)
Three layers:
1. "The Joker cannot win" — If Dent's corruption is revealed, 549 arrests collapse. Joker wins retroactively.
2. "I'm not a hero, like Dent. I killed those people. That's what I can be." — Becomes the VILLAIN so Gotham keeps its HERO.
3. "I'm whatever Gotham needs me to be." — Identity death. Not just taking blame — becoming what's needed.

**Supporting Beats (Final Montage — MOVEMENT cascade):**
- Gordon destroys bat-signal with axe (p. 140)
- Alfred burns Rachel's letter by firelight (p. 141)
- Fox types his name — sonar self-destructs (p. 141)
- Batman limps to Bat-Pod, rides into blinding light (p. 141)
- "Because we have to chase him." (p. 140)

**What changed from v1.0:** "Forced irreversibility / takes the blame" → Three-layer sacrifice + MOVEMENT as contagious (four characters all make irreversible choices in the montage). "You either die a hero or live long enough to see yourself become the villain" correctly placed HERE (finale), not at the hospital.
